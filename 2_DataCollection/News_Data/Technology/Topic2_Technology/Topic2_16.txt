We’re adding the stories of remarkable women like Ida B. Wells, who took on racism in the South.    Audio    Follow The Daily:  Apple Podcasts  Google Play Music  Radio Public  RSS Feed    Ida B. Wells was an investigative reporter who exposed the systematic lynching of black men in the South. Her work made her the most famous black woman in the country. But when she died in 1931, at the age of 68, The New York Times failed to write an obituary. Obituaries in The Times have been long dominated by white men. Now, the paper of record is trying to fix the record. On today’s episode: • Amisha Padnani, the digital editor on The Times’s obituaries desk and a leader of the Overlooked project.
